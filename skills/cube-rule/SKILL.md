# The Cube Rule of Food Identification

The Cube Rule classifies all foods purely by the position of structural starch (carbohydrate) relative to the filling. Using the six faces of a cube as a model, any food can be categorized by which faces are starch. Created by @Phosphatide as the grand unified theory of food identification. Reference: https://cuberule.com/

## Classification System

| # | Category | Starch Position | Examples |
|---|----------|----------------|----------|
| 0 | Salad | No structural starch | Steak, mashed potatoes, soup ("wet salad"), flan, chocolate, turducken |
| 1 | Toast | Bottom only | Pizza, nigiri sushi, pie slice (bent toast), muffin/bread block (unsliced) |
| 2 | Sandwich | Top + bottom | Quesadilla (non-folded), toast sandwich, victoria sponge cake |
| 3 | Taco | Bottom + two sides | Hot dog, sub sandwich (uncut), pie slice (on its side) |
| 4 | Sushi | Bottom + top + two sides | Falafel wrap, pigs in a blanket, enchilada |
| 5 | Quiche | Bottom + all four sides (open top) | Cheesecake, soup in bread bowl, falafel pita |
| 6 | Calzone | All six sides enclosed | Burrito, corn dog, whole pie, dumplings, pop-tarts, uncrustables |
| 7 | Cake | Three stacked starch layers | Lasagna, Big Mac, flapjacks/pancake stack |
| 8 | Nachos | Smaller solid starch pieces within | Poutine, Lucky Charms, salad with croutons, ramen ("wet nachos"), couscous |

## How to Classify

1. **Identify the structural starch** - bread, tortilla, pastry, noodle sheets, etc.
2. **Determine which faces of the cube the starch occupies** - bottom, top, sides, or combinations
3. **Match to the category** using the table above
4. If there is no structural starch at all, it is a **salad** (category 0)
5. If starch exists as smaller pieces mixed in rather than forming structure, it is **nachos** (category 8)

## Special Rules

- **Rice:** Interpreters have complete freedom in defining its nature as structural starch or filling. This is intentional ambiguity.
- **Vanilla soy latte:** A three-bean soup. Soup has no structural starch. Therefore it is a wet salad (category 0).
- **Muffins/bread blocks:** Toast (category 1) in raw, unsliced form - starch is only on the bottom.
- **Humans:** Ravioli (per food critic Soleil Ho). Skin encloses filling on all sides = calzone (category 6).
- **Starch orientation matters:** A sub sandwich becomes a taco (category 3) if uncut, because the bread wraps bottom + two sides. If cut in half and separated, each half is toast (category 1).

## Usage

When classifying a food:
1. State the classification clearly with category number and name
2. Explain the reasoning - where is the structural starch?
3. Handle absurdist edge cases enthusiastically - the cube rule's charm is in pushing it to its logical extremes
4. Acknowledge legitimate ambiguity (like rice) when it exists
